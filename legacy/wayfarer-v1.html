Turn your trip photos into a real travel diary

Drop in photos from your trip. Wayfarer reads the location baked into each photo, looks up where you were, and writes the history and culture of the place next to your pictures — organized into chapters you can edit, save, and share.

Have your trip in Google Photos instead? import

Wayfarer runs entirely in your browser and can't sign in to your Google account on its own, so it can't reach into a private Google Photos library directly. Two ways to bring photos in from there:

Option A (recommended): open the album, select Download all (or the photos you want), then drag those downloaded files into the box above — this keeps the original GPS/date info Wayfarer reads automatically.
Option B: paste a direct image link below (right-click a photo in the album → “Copy image address”) and Wayfarer will try to pull it in directly — this sometimes works for public albums, but Google usually strips location data from shared-link images, so you may need to set the place manually.
